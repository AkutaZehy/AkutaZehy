<mark>Python is a language that I used to use but hate at the same time. Although I have studied and worked in deep learning so far, if given the chance, I will not use it in the future. </mark>

One of the reasons is shown in this passage: 
[Dear conda, drop DEAD](https://akutazehy.github.io/morsel/posts/conda%E4%BD%A0%E7%9C%9F%E7%9A%84%E5%8F%AF%E4%BB%A5%E5%8E%BB%E6%AD%BB%E4%BA%86/) (Written in Chinese)

I am extremely pessimistic about the development of AI in small companies. When you think about [FAIR](https://github.com/facebookresearch), a group training models with 100 [A100](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/data-center/a100/) graphics cards in 2023, you know that unless you are a super genius, it is something you can never catch up with in your lifetime without *enough* money—I am not the kind, and the rest part, the part everyone could catch up, have long been devoured up by the market.

It *is* a good language as a tool, portable and easy to execute, especially for deep learning projects, much better than MATLAB, R or Fortran; but for a slightly complicated project including many imports, it is indeed a bad language with unbearable syntactic sugar, casual variable type and terrible readability.
